# Tone Consistency System

This document defines the tone framework and evaluation model used to ensure consistent, high-quality product content.

---

## Tone Principles

All product content should follow these four core principles:

### 1. Clear
- Use simple, direct language
- Avoid jargon and technical complexity
- Make actions and outcomes obvious

✅ Example:  
"Enter your email to continue"

❌ Example:  
"Kindly provide your electronic mail address to proceed"

---

### 2. Reassuring
- Reduce user anxiety
- Acknowledge issues without blame
- Provide a sense of control

✅ Example:  
"Something went wrong. Please try again."

❌ Example:  
"Error: request failed"

---

### 3. Concise
- Keep messages short and focused
- Remove unnecessary words
- Prioritize key information

✅ Example:  
"Payment failed. Try another method."

❌ Example:  
"Unfortunately, your payment could not be processed at this time"

---

### 4. Human
- Use a conversational tone
- Avoid robotic or overly formal phrasing
- Be friendly but not overly casual

✅ Example:  
"We couldn’t find your booking"

❌ Example:  
"Oopsie! We totally lost your booking 😅"

---

## Tone Boundaries

Maintain balance:
- Not too formal  
- Not too casual  
- Not overly emotional  
- Not overly technical  

---

## Evaluation Model

Each piece of content is evaluated across 4 dimensions:

| Dimension     | Description |
|--------------|------------|
| Clarity       | Is the message easy to understand? |
| Tone          | Does it align with defined voice? |
| Conciseness   | Is it free of unnecessary words? |
| Actionability | Does it guide the user clearly? |

---

## Scoring System

Each dimension is scored from 1 to 5:

- 1 = Poor
- 3 = Acceptable
- 5 = Excellent

---

## Common Issues

- Overly formal language  
- Lack of clear next step  
- Technical jargon  
- Inconsistent tone across flows  
- Messages that blame the user  

---

## Content Patterns

### Error Messages
- What happened  
- Why (if helpful)  
- What to do next  

### Success Messages
- Confirm action  
- Reinforce confidence  

### Empty States
- Explain context  
- Suggest next action  

---

## Key Principle

Content consistency is achieved through systems, not individual writing decisions.
